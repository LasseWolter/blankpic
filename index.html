#nopic

Our idea

#nopic stands for omitting photos in the professional context. The way you look shouldn't impact your chances of getting hired. 
We want to fight this discrimination and make a statement by replacing photos with #nopic.
If you'd like to join us, get your #nopic down below and add it to your CV, LinkedIn, Website, etc.

FAQ

Are you against pictures in general?

No! Pictures are a great way to share emotions and help to develop personal relationships. However, we believe they aren't suitable for the professional context. Furthermore, there is lots of software available to manipulate photos. Everybody can decide whether one wants to use such tools for private purposes but they should not influence whether someone gets hired.

Isn't it more personal to include a photo? 

It may. However, being honest, many photos do not show who a person really is. We believe, personal relationships are based on real-world interactions. Additionally, the time in which CVs are reviewed is usually too short leave a personal impression which goes beyond the mere appearance of a person. 

Why use #nopic instead of no pic at all?

Using no photo is a great statement against discrimination! However, people who come across your CV or your online presence don't know about your reasons for not using a photo. This could even cause you to have disadvantages. Putting #nopic clearly explains why you do not have a picture and will hopefully make other people think about this issue.

What about LinkedIn or Xing?

Enterprise social networking is the modern way of connecting in the working world. Thus, we recommend putting a #nopic on those too.

But anybody can find pictures of me on social media. Why does it matter whether I use them in a professional context?

Not everyone who comes across your CV or online presence will search social media to find photos of you. This especially applies to situations where people have to review lots of applications in a short amount of time. 

Do you claim that everybody is prejudiced?

Yes and no. We do not claim that people willingly discriminate others because of their photos. However, subconsciously all of us have beliefs that influence our decisions even though we are not aware of it. In large companies applications are reviewed in a very short amount of time. Hence, reviewers don't have the time to question their subconscious impressions.
